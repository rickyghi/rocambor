# Rocambor / Tresillo - Game Rules

## Overview

Rocambor (also known as Tresillo) is a classic Spanish trick-taking card game. It uses the Spanish 40-card deck and features an auction system where players compete to become the **Ombre** (declarer).

## The Deck

The **Spanish deck** has 40 cards across 4 suits:

| Suit     | Symbol | Color  |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Oros     | Coins  | Gold   |
| Copas    | Cups   | Red    |
| Espadas  | Swords | Silver |
| Bastos   | Clubs  | Green  |

Each suit has 10 cards: 1 (Ace), 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 10 (Sota/Jack), 11 (Caballo/Knight), 12 (Rey/King).

> Note: Ranks 8 and 9 do not exist in the Spanish deck.

## Game Modes

### Tresillo (3 Players)
- 3 active players, 1 rests each hand (rotates)
- 9 cards dealt per player, 13 in the talon (draw pile)
- 9 tricks per hand

### Quadrille (4 Players)
- 4 active players (the 4th rotates resting each hand)
- 8 cards dealt per player, 8 in the talon
- 8 tricks per hand

## Card Ranking

### Trump Suit

The top 3 cards in trump are called **Matadores** and always rank highest:

1. **Spadille** - Ace of Espadas (always the #1 trump, regardless of chosen trump suit)
2. **Manille** - The 7 of the trump suit (for black suits: Espadas/Bastos) OR the 2 of the trump suit (for red suits: Oros/Copas)
3. **Basto** - Ace of Bastos (always the #3 trump, regardless of chosen trump suit)

After the matadores, remaining trump cards rank: King (12), Knight (11), Jack (10), 7/6/5/4/3/2 (varies by suit color).

### Non-Trump Suits

For **red suits** (Oros, Copas): King > Knight > Jack > Ace > 2 > 3 > 4 > 5 > 6 > 7

For **black suits** (Espadas, Bastos): King > Knight > Jack > Ace > 2 > 3 > 4 > 5 > 6 > 7

> Note: When Espadas or Bastos is NOT trump, their Aces are still powerful but rank normally within their suit.

## Phases of Play

### 1. Dealing

Cards are dealt from a shuffled deck. One player rests each hand (in rotation).

### 2. Auction

Players bid to become the Ombre. Available bids (from lowest to highest):

| Bid       | Value | Description                                    |
|-----------|-------|------------------------------------------------|
| Pass      | 0     | Decline to bid                                 |
| Entrada   | 1     | Basic declaration, choose any trump             |
| Oros      | 2     | Must choose Oros as trump                       |
| Volteo    | 3     | Higher bid, any trump                           |
| Solo      | 4     | Play without exchanging cards                   |
| Solo Oros | 5     | Solo with Oros as trump                         |
| Bola      | 6     | Must win ALL tricks                             |

Each player must bid higher than the current bid or pass. The highest bidder becomes the Ombre.

**Espada Obligatoria**: If all players pass, the holder of the Spadille (Ace of Espadas) must bid Entrada. If the Spadille is in the talon, the deal passes.

### 3. Trump Choice

The Ombre chooses the trump suit (unless the contract forces Oros).

### 4. Exchange

Unless playing Solo or Bola:
- The **Ombre** may discard cards and draw replacements from the talon (up to 8 cards)
- Other active players may also exchange in turn order

### 5. Play

Players play tricks in turn order:
- **Lead player** plays any card
- Subsequent players must **follow suit** if possible
- If unable to follow suit and holding trump, players **must trump**
- If unable to follow suit or trump, any card may be played
- The highest trump wins, or if no trump, the highest card of the led suit

### 6. Scoring

The Ombre needs to win **more tricks than any single opponent** to succeed.

#### Sacada (Success)
The Ombre wins at least 5 tricks (in Tresillo with 9 tricks). Points awarded based on contract value.

#### Codille (Defeat)
An opponent wins more tricks than the Ombre. The Ombre loses points, and the winning opponent gains points.

#### Puesta (Draw)
The Ombre ties with an opponent (or no one reaches 5). The Ombre loses a reduced penalty.

#### Special Outcomes

- **Bola Made**: The Ombre wins all 9 tricks. Large bonus.
- **Bola Failed**: The Ombre declared Bola but failed. Large penalty.
- **Contrabola**: An opponent declares they will win all remaining tricks.
- **Oros Bonus**: Extra points when Oros is trump (for Oros/Solo Oros contracts).
- **Penetro**: When no active player holds the Spadille (it is in the talon) and all pass, a special forced game occurs.

## Scoring Table

|---------------|----------------|--------------------|

| Bola Failed   | -10            | +10 each           |
| Oros Bonus    | +1 extra       | -1 extra each      |

## Winning

The first player to reach the **target score** (default: 12 points) wins the match.

## Strategy Tips

- Matadores are extremely powerful - having 2+ usually warrants a bid
- Solo contracts are risky but highly rewarding
- Watch which suits opponents are short in during the exchange
- The Spadille (Ace of Espadas) is always the strongest card in the game
- Oros contracts give a bonus but telegraph your trump to opponents
